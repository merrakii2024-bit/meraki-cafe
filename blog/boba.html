What is "Real" Boba — and Why Does It Matter?

Boba originated in Taiwan, and at its heart, it is a tea-forward experience. A great cup relies on three things: the quality of the tea leaves, the freshness of the tapioca pearls, and the balance of the milk-to-tea ratio.

True boba has a defining characteristic: the "QQ" texture. That signature, springy, chewy bite of a perfectly cooked tapioca pearl is the hallmark of true boba. When you drink our boba, you aren't just tasting sugar; you are tasting the distinct notes of premium, freshly brewed tea leaves, combined with the silkiness of real milk.

"Great Boba is a texture-first experience. It isn't about masking the tea — it is about elevating it. We don't use powders; we brew it to be savoured." — Meraki Café Kitchen Team

The Fresh-Brewed Philosophy — Behind the Counter

Most boba shops use instant tea powders and store-bought syrups. It works. But it cannot produce the flavour or the clean finish that fresh-brewed tea creates. Here is what happens in our kitchen:

1

Freshly Brewed Tea Bases

Our boba begins with loose-leaf tea. We avoid instant tea powders entirely. We steep our tea at the precise temperature to extract the rich, aromatic profile of the leaf without the bitterness.

2

The "Perfect Chew" Pearl

A stale pearl is rubbery; a properly cooked one is silky on the outside and firm on the inside. We cook our pearls in small batches throughout the day to ensure the texture is always fresh.

3

House-Made Sweetness

We avoid cloying artificial sweeteners. We use house-made simple syrups and quality milk to create a drink that is refreshing, not heavy. You should taste the creaminess, the tea, and the sweetness—in that order.
